About Hadiya Zone

this page is about Hadiya zone

Hadiya Zone is a dynamic and culturally rich administrative zone in the Central Ethiopia Regional State, Ethiopia. Its capital and largest city is Hosanna (also spelled Hosaena or Hossana), which serves as the administrative hub and a growing center for commerce, education, agriculture, and regional governance.

Hosanna is a vibrant town located about 230 km south of Addis Ababa, at an elevation of around 2,177 meters. It has experienced rapid growth and serves as the seat of both Hadiya Zone administration and the Central Ethiopia Regional State.

Leadership & Governance

Hadiya Zone operates under the Central Ethiopia Regional State, led by His Excellency Dr. Endashaw Tassew as President. The zone is administered by a dedicated team focused on local development, agriculture, infrastructure, education, and prosperity for the Hadiya people.

For the latest updates and official announcements, visit: Hadiya Zone Administration Website

  • Capital: Hosanna
  • Key Features: Agriculture (especially enset, grains, coffee), Education hubs, Growing urban development, Cultural heritage of the Hadiya people
  • Population: Over 1.5 million (zone-wide estimate)
