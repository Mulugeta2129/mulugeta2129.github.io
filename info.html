About Hadiya Zone

Hadiya Zone is a vibrant administrative zone in the Central Ethiopia Regional State, Ethiopia. It is home to the Hadiya people and known for its agricultural richness (especially enset, grains, and coffee), cultural heritage, educational institutions, and growing urban development. The zone plays a key role in regional prosperity through sustainable development, infrastructure, and community initiatives.

Hosanna – The Capital City

Hosanna (also spelled Hosaena or Hossana) is the capital and largest town of Hadiya Zone, serving as the administrative center for both the zone and parts of the regional state. Located approximately 230 km south of Addis Ababa at an elevation of about 2,177 meters, it is a fast-growing hub for commerce, education, and governance.

Leadership & Governance

Hadiya Zone operates under the Central Ethiopia Regional State, led by His Excellency Dr. Endashaw Tassew as President/Chief Administrator of the region (since 2023). The zone is administered by a dedicated zonal administrator focused on local development, agriculture, infrastructure, education, and prosperity for the Hadiya people.

For the most current leadership information and announcements, visit the official website: Hadiya Zone Administration.

  • Capital: Hosanna
  • Key Sectors: Agriculture, Education, Trade, Tourism, Infrastructure
  • Administrative Structure: 13 woredas, 7 towns, under Central Ethiopia Regional State
  • Population: Estimated over 1.5 million (zone-wide)
