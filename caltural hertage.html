Traditional Dances

Hadiya people are renowned for energetic group dances such as Boyaa Seleme and other rhythmic performances performed during weddings, festivals, and important ceremonies. These dances express joy, unity, strength, and community solidarity.

Traditional Clothing & Adornment

Women wear beautifully embroidered dresses with bright colors and intricate patterns, often complemented by beadwork, necklaces, and headscarves. Men traditionally wear gabi or embroidered shawls and carry ceremonial sticks during important events.

Yahode – Hadiya New Year

The most important cultural festival, Yahode (or Yahoode), marks renewal, peace, reconciliation, and thanksgiving. It features feasting, traditional foods, music, dances, and family gatherings.

Oral Tradition & Storytelling

Elders play a central role in preserving history through storytelling, proverbs, riddles, and folk songs. These oral traditions transmit values, genealogy, moral lessons, and historical events across generations.

“The Hadiya people value hospitality, respect for elders, strong family ties, hard work, and peaceful coexistence — core principles deeply embedded in daily life and cultural practices.”

Musical Instruments & Arts

  • Drums (Dibbe / Dibe) — different sizes used in ceremonies and dances
  • Krar and other string instruments in some performances
  • Washint (bamboo flute) — used in pastoral and ceremonial contexts
  • Handclapping, ululation, and group singing are essential elements
  • Pottery, weaving, and basketry remain important traditional crafts
